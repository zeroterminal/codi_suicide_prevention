A suicide attempt is when someone harms themselves with any intent to end their life, but they do not die as a result of their actions.

Warning Signs:

Warning Signs help the visitors recognize potential and early signs in themselves or others and encourage early intervention
Talking about suicide: expressing the thoughts of wanting to die or hopelessness
Getting the means to take your own life: such as buying a gun or stockpiling pills
Having mood swings: extreme shifts in moods from being happy to being sad in a short period of time or vice-versa
Isolation: stops attending social events or social gatherings with family and friends
Loss of interest: loss of interest in previously enjoyed activities or hobbies
Changing daily routine: such as eating or sleeping patterns

Risk Factors:

Risk factors, which may be individual or environmental, can increase the likelihood of suicidal behavior.
Attempted suicide before
Feeling hopeless, worthless, or socially isolated
Experiencing a stressful life event, such as the loss of a loved one, a breakup, or financial or legal problems
Having suicidal thoughts and having access to firearms in your home
Having an underlying psychiatric disorder, such as major depression, or post-traumatic stress disorder
Having a family history of mental disorders, suicide, or violence, including physical or sexual abuse
Having a medical condition that can be linked to depression and suicidal thinking, such as chronic disease or terminal illness

Suicide Prevention Methods:

Suicide prevention methods and treatment are based on patient risk factors
Speaking Up: whether the individual has suicidal thoughts, or you spot the warning signs in someone else, you must speak up
Psychotherapy or talk therapy; most common
Cognitive Behavioral Therapy: individuals are taught new ways of dealing with stress and stressful life experiences instead of directing to take their own life
Dialectical Behavior Therapy: individuals are taught to recognize disruptive or unhealthy feelings or actions
Increased education and awareness among medical professionals might decrease suicide rates in the future
