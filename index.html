Learn More – Get Educated Understanding Suicide: Suicide is the act of intentionally causing one’s own death. what is suicide, why it happens, and the factors that contribute to it? Warning Signs: Common warning signs that someone may be struggling with suicidal thoughts. This helps the visitors recognize potential signs in themselves or others and encourages early intervention. Risk Factors: explain the risk factors (individual/environmental) that can increase the likelihood of suicidal behavior. Protective Factors: explain the factors that can provide protection against suicide. Myths vs. Facts: address common misconceptions and myths about suicide  reduce stigma. Importance of Seeking Help: emphasize the significance of reaching out for help if someone is struggling with suicidal thoughts. (how to get professional help) Self-Care Strategies: share strategies for self-care and emotional well-being. Offer practical tips for managing stress, seeking support, and promoting mental health. Support for Families and Friends: Provide guidance for friends and family members on how to offer support and communicate with someone who might be struggling. Additionally, provide help for those who experienced the loss of a loved one due to suicide. OUR GOAL • Reduce the Suicide Attempt Rate. • Increase Communication efforts via all social media that promote positive messages and support safe intervention strategies. • Increase Knowledge of warning signs for suicide. • Increase knowledge of the factors that offer protection from suicidal behaviors and that promote wellness and recovery. • Extend care and support to individuals who are experiencing thoughts of suicide. • Provide care and support to individuals affected by suicide deaths and attempt to promote healing and implement community strategies to help prevent further suicides.  WE CAN ADD SOMETHING IN THE CONTACT US PART RELATED TO THE INDIVIDUALS AFFECTED BY SUICIDE!
